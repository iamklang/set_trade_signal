# US Earnings-Season Overlay

During earnings season, results become the dominant short-term variable. Layer
this on top of chart and macro.

> Not financial advice. Consensus, report dates, and read-throughs are forecasts/
> inference — verify before acting.

## Build an earnings calendar

For each watchlist name, know:
- **Expected report date + time** (BMO before-open / AMC after-close)
- **Reported or not**
- **Consensus** EPS & revenue, and the **whisper** number
- Tag: **beat / in-line / miss**, plus the reaction driver

## The US truth: guidance > the print

US stocks trade on **forward guidance and the conference call**, not just the
headline beat. A beat with weak guidance sells off; a miss with raised guidance
rallies. Read the *reaction to the guidance*, not the EPS line alone.

## Classify the setup

### Reported + beat
- Watch for **sell-the-news**: a good print already priced in can open red,
  especially in crowded mega-caps.
- Don't chase the spike — enter on a clean **dip to support / the 20-EMA**, or
  on a **gap-up that holds** (base-and-go).

### Reported + miss
- **Washout / capitulation play**: dumped on volume, absorbed over 2–3 days down
  to an old base where volume dries up.
- Buy there **only if** the miss is one-off (weather, timing, one segment) and
  the franchise + guidance are intact — not if guidance was cut and the growth
  story broke. Don't average down a broken base.

### Not yet reported
- **Pre-position** only for a laggard at base / below the EMA where you judge the
  print won't be bad — limited downside at support, always with a stop.
- **Follow** (buy after on confirmation): if you can't handicap the print, wait,
  let it gap and hold, then follow.

## Peer read-through (sector dominoes)

When the first mover in a group prints, **infer** the read-through for later
names (e.g. one hyperscaler's capex guide moves the whole semi/AI-power chain;
a bank kickoff sets the tone for financials; a retailer's consumer read moves the
group). Flag it as *inference*, not fact.

## Print-day risk rule

*If you can't stomach a 10%+ gap up AND down, be flat before the report.* US
single-stock earnings gaps are large. If holding through, pre-define the stop and
don't negotiate it — and size for a gap-through-stop (slippage), not the stop.

## IV crush — the options earnings trap

- Option implied volatility is bid **up** into a known earnings date and collapses
  **immediately after** (IV crush).
- A call/put bought right before the print is **expensive**; even a correct
  directional call can lose once IV deflates post-announcement.
- Prefer to **enter options *after* the print** (follow the confirmed move), or
  express the pre-print view in **stock** or a **longer-dated / lower-IV-rank**
  option. See `/us-options` for structure choice.
- Check **IV rank/percentile** and the **expected move** (from the straddle)
  before buying premium into a print.

## Layer macro over earnings

Even a great print gets sold if the macro flips (hot CPI, hawkish Fed, yields
spike) — the tape overrides the single name. Check the calendar (CPI/FOMC) before
sizing an earnings trade.

- "Is this a sell-the-news or a real breakout after earnings?"

- "Peer read-through: one hyperscaler guided up — what about the others?"
