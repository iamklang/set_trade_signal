# SET Earnings-Season Overlay (งบ)

During earnings season, results become the dominant short-term variable. Layer
this on top of chart and macro — "there are more variables than just looking
at earnings."

> Not financial advice. Consensus, report dates, and peer read-throughs are
> forecasts/inference — verify before acting.

## Build an earnings calendar

For each watchlist name, know:
- **Expected report date**
- **Reported or not** (`งบออกแล้ว` vs `ยังไม่ออก`)
- **Consensus vs actual** (or vs last year if uncovered)
- Tag: **beat / in-line / miss**, with YoY%, turnaround/record, dividend/XD

## Classify the setup

### Reported + beat
- Watch for **sell-on-fact (`เซลล์ on fact`)**: good งบ does NOT guarantee a
  green candle (leaders can open red).
- Don't chase the spike — only enter on a clean **dip to support / the orange
  EMA**.

### Reported + miss
- **Selling-Climax (SC) play**: the stock is dumped on volume, absorbed over
  2–3 days (`ซับแรงขาย`) down to an old base where volume dries up and bots
  accumulate.
- Buy there **only if** the business still profits / pays a dividend — a high
  still-payable yield at the lower price can justify it.
- **Distinguish a miss-dump from a broken business:** a one-off miss is often
  an SC buy at the old base; genuine growth deterioration eventually breaks the
  base — don't blindly average down (`ถัวกันหน้ามืด`).

### Not yet reported
Two choices:
- **Pre-position (buy before on expectation):** only for a laggard at base /
  below the EMA where you judge งบ won't be bad — downside is limited at the
  base. Always with a **structural stop**.
- **Follow (buy after on confirmation):** if you can't judge the print,
  **wait, let it gap up, then follow** the move.

## Peer read-through (sector dominoes)

When early/small names in a group beat, **infer** the bigger/later names will
follow — set alerts on the group's bellwether print (e.g. refiners follow the
leader; retail follows the leader; the leasing pack moves together).

This is *inference*, not reported fact — flag it.

## Print-day risk rule

*If you can't stomach a gap up AND a gap down, be flat before the report.*
If holding through, pre-define the stop at the structural level and don't
negotiate it.

## IV crush — the DW-specific earnings trap

- Issuers mark a DW's implied volatility *up* into a known earnings date and
  *down* immediately after.
- A DW bought right before the print is **expensive (inflated IV)**, and even
  a correct directional call can lose money once IV deflates post-announcement.
- Prefer to **enter DWs *after* the print** (follow the confirmed move) rather
  than buy a pumped-IV DW before it.
- If you must pre-position, do it in the **underlying stock** or a
  **low-IV/longer-dated DW**.

## Layer macro over งบ

Even good earnings can be dumped if the macro flips (e.g. peace breaks, money
rotates out of war-beneficiaries) — `งบดีแล้วโดนเทแบบงงๆ`. Check war/peace +
key meetings before sizing war-sensitive names.
